## Rationale

Direct uplift is modest — extra living area doesn't jump comp pools the way bed/bath count does —
but this is the **enabler move**: it banks 21.8 m² of internal area under the existing roofline
and replaces the living amenity that the next version will take (lounge → Bedroom 5). Sequencing
matters: converting the lounge *without* this replacement living zone would leave a 5-bed house
with almost no living space, which rents poorly. This is the "enclose balcony / new living /
move walls" move from your original hand concept, staged safely.

## Compliance flags (advisory)

**Pathway: specialist first** — the wall between balcony and kitchen/dining is almost certainly
load-bearing under the main roof; a structural engineer sizes the opening/beam before the approval
pathway (likely CDC/DA) is knowable.

- Former balcony floor: waterproofing history, floor-level step, and insulation to habitable
  standard; the balustrade edge becomes a glazed external wall (BASIX glazing requirements).
- Dining and kitchen will borrow natural light through the enclosed zone — re-check light (~10%)
  and ventilation (~5%) ratios for those rooms.
- The external stair off the balcony is absorbed by the enclosure — retain/relocate yard access
  and confirm egress.
